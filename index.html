Help

The controls boil down to: choose your angle and speed (i.e. time, in ms, between drawing)

Controls/Buttons:

Reset
Reset to initial state.
Draw
Draw the next rotation.
Play
Keep drawing rotations.

Shortcuts:

n
Jump to the n-th rotation. NOTE: this doesn't include all of the rotations between the current rotation and n. It sets n to the given input.
d
Draw the next rotation
<spacebar>
Toggle the play button
0
Set the time between draws to 0; i.e. draw as fast as you can!
-
Decrease the amount of time between draws.
+
Increase the amount of time between draws.

About

Hey there! I'm André. I'm a Ph.D. student at Wesleyan University and my research is in Diophantine Approximations. You can read more about it on my website.

Some aspects of this field revolve around (ha.) looking at the rotations (mod 1) of a real number.

Similar to looking at the integers $\pmod n$, a number $\pmod 1$ is its fractional part (e.g. $\pi = 3.14159...$ (mod 1) is $.14159...$). I like to image taking the real line, taking all of the intervals $[n, n+1)$ and stacking them above the unit interval $[0,1)$. Doing this identifies the endpoints, 0 and 1; So that we're actually on a circle!

There will be a blog post explaining more of this soon!

The red dash is $0$ (or 1). Each green dash is the fractional part of $n \alpha$ for some $n$ and the chosen $\alpha$. The orange/yellow dashes are "record breaker" rotations. Specifically, they correspond to the $n \alpha$ that gets the closest to 0 out of all of the previously visited values. The length of the dash corresponds to the value of $n$; it was hard to distinguish different dashes once they started overlapping.
